defeated Enigma but not homophobia

Alan Turing life's milestone

  • 1912 - Born in London
  • 1935 - Is elected a Fellow at King's College, Cambridge.
  • 1936 - He writes "On Computable Numbers..." where he describes the Turing Machine
  • 1939 - Moves to Bletchley Park, where he works on deciphering Nazi messages
  • 1939/42 - Builds the "Turing Bombs," the machine that hacked Enigma
  • 1945 - Works at the National Physical Laboratory
  • 1946 - Begins the construction of Automatic Computing Engine or ACE.
  • 1948 - Director of the Calculus Laboratory at University of Manchester.
  • 1950 - Publishes "Computing Machinery and Intelligence" in the philosophical journal Mind.
  • 1951 - Fellow of the Royal Society. Publishes work on non - linear Morphogenesis theory
  • 1952 - He is arrested and convicted as a homosexual. Undergoes chemical castration to avoid jail time
  • 1954 - Dies by suicide in Wilmslow, Cheshire; an apple poisoned with cyanide is found near his body.
